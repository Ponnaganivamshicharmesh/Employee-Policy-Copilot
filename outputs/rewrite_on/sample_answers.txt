Q: What is the travel reimbursement policy?
Retrieved: ['finance/travel_reimbursement_policy', 'finance/meal_allowance_policy', 'finance/expense_policy']
Expected: ['finance/travel_reimbursement_policy']
Answer: The Travel Reimbursement Policy (Version 1.2) defines reimbursable business travel expenses and the process for claiming them. It applies to employees who travel on approved company business. 

Eligible expenses for reimbursement include:
- Economy airfare
- Train tickets
- Local transportation
- Hotel accommodation
- Meals, all within approved daily limits

Specific details include:
- Meal expenses are reimbursable, capped at 800 INR per day for domestic travel, unless pre-approved for a higher limit.
- All travel must be approved in advance by the reporting manager and, where applicable, the budget owner.
- Claims must be submitted within 15 calendar days of trip completion through the expense system with itemised receipts.

Exceptions to the policy require written approval from the department head and Finance. Ride-sharing services are reimbursable when used for local travel during an approved trip and supported by receipts.

Q: Are meal expenses reimbursable, and what is the daily limit?

Expected: ['finance/travel_reimbursement_policy', 'finance/meal_allowance_policy']
Answer: Yes, meal expenses are reimbursable. For domestic travel, the daily limit is 800 INR per day, excluding alcohol. For international travel, meal limits are defined by destination and published annually by Finance.
